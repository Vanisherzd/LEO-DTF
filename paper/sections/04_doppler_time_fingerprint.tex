\section{Doppler-Time Fingerprint}\label{sec:dtf}

\subsection{Fingerprint as a Geometric Signature}
The Doppler-time fingerprint is the functional relationship
$f_D(t; \mathbf{x})$ induced by candidate position $\mathbf{x}$ over the
duration of a satellite pass.  Because the range-rate $\dot{\rho}(t; \mathbf{x})$
depends on the terminal-satellite geometry, two distinct positions inside the
ROI generally produce measurably different Doppler trajectories.  The
\textbf{shape} of this trajectory---its curvature, peak magnitude, and
zero-crossing time---constitutes a position-dependent fingerprint.

Define the discrete Doppler fingerprint vector over $N$ observation epochs:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{g}(\mathbf{x})
  = \bigl[
      f_D(t_1; \mathbf{x}),\;
      f_D(t_2; \mathbf{x}),\;
      \ldots,\;
      f_D(t_N; \mathbf{x})
    \bigr]^{\top}
  \in \mathbb{R}^{N}.
  \label{eq:doppler_fingerprint}
\end{equation}
If delay observations are available, the corresponding delay fingerprint is
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{h}(\mathbf{x})
  = \bigl[
      \rho(t_1; \mathbf{x})/c,\;
      \ldots,\;
      \rho(t_N; \mathbf{x})/c
    \bigr]^{\top}
  \in \mathbb{R}^{N}.
  \label{eq:delay_fingerprint}
\end{equation}
The full observation vector is $\mathbf{y} = [\mathbf{y}_f^{\top},\mathbf{y}_\tau^{\top}]^{\top}$
when both modalities are used, or $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_f$ for frequency-only.

\subsection{Sensitivity to Nuisance Parameters}
The observed frequency at each epoch is
\begin{equation}
  y_f(t_i) = f_D(t_i; \mathbf{x}) + b_0 + b_1\,t_i + \epsilon_i ,
\end{equation}
so the nuisance terms affect the fingerprint as follows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $b_0$ adds a constant vertical offset to $\mathbf{g}(\mathbf{x})$.
  \item $b_1$ adds a linear tilt across the time axis of $\mathbf{g}(\mathbf{x})$.
  \item $\Delta t$ shifts the delay fingerprint $\mathbf{h}(\mathbf{x})$ and has
        a secondary effect on Doppler via the range at each shifted time.
  \item $\epsilon_i$ adds independent Gaussian noise, blurring the posterior.
\end{itemize}
These effects are degenerate with position: a vertical shift can be compensated
by moving the candidate location, and a linear tilt can mimic the Doppler curve
of a different ground track.  Joint estimation of $\mathbf{x}$ and
$\boldsymbol{\theta}$ is required to disambiguate geometry from oscillator
state.

\subsection{Multi-Epoch Discriminability}
A single snapshot observation provides at most one scalar constraint
(range-rate) on two unknown position coordinates, making the problem
underdetermined.  Moreover, the scalar Doppler $f_D$ is approximately linear
in the lateral position coordinate near the sub-satellite track, creating
a left-right ambiguity.  Tracking the Doppler curve over a 5--10 minute pass
samples the geometry at multiple elevation angles, substantially increasing
the rank of the observation.  The multi-sample fingerprint in
\eqref{eq:doppler_fingerprint} has discriminative power only when the Doppler
trajectory shape---not just its value at one instant---is used.

This motivates the grid-search posterior approach rather than a closed-form
solution; it also explains why the CRLB for position from Doppler-only
observations can be large when the ROI spans hundreds of kilometers.

A simple Mahalanobis diagnostic for the separability of two candidates
$\mathbf{x}_i$ and $\mathbf{x}_j$ is:
\begin{equation}
  d_{ij}
  = \min_{\theta_i,\theta_j}
    \bigl\|
      \boldsymbol{\mu}(\mathbf{x}_i, \theta_i)
      - \boldsymbol{\mu}(\mathbf{x}_j, \theta_j)
    \bigr\|_{\Sigma^{-1}} ,
  \label{eq:fingerprint_distance}
\end{equation}
where $\boldsymbol{\mu}(\mathbf{x},\theta)$ is the modeled observation vector
(frequency and optionally delay) under nuisance parameter $\theta$, and
$\Sigma$ is the observation noise covariance.
Larger $d_{ij}$ indicates greater discriminability; $d_{ij}\lesssim 1$ suggests
that candidates $i$ and $j$ are near-degenerate given the noise level.
This is a heuristic diagnostic, not a rigorous identifiability condition.
